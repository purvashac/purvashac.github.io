Purvasha Chakravarti

About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Statistics and Data Science at Carnegie Mellon University. Previously, I have obtained a Masters in Machine Learning from CMU and a Bachelors in Statistics from the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata.

I am currently working with Larry Wasserman and Sivaraman Balakrishnan on developing a clustering algorithm that comes equipped with a significance guarantee.

Research Interests

My research interests span theory and algorithms in the fields of statistics and machine learning. Specifically, it includes developing clustering and density estimation algorithms and applying them to science and technology.

In my current work with Larry Wasserman and Sivaraman Balakrishnan, we develop a test for whether a mixture of Gaussians provides a better fit to the data relative to a single Gaussian and use this test to develop a clustering algorithm. We show how the test can be used in a hierarchical as well as a sequential manner for clustering.

As a part of my Ph.D. course work, for my Advanced Data Analysis Project, I worked with Bill Eddy. We developed a multi-country compartment model and a multi-country time series model to predict the spread of Chikungunya in the Americas.

I have also worked on developing a convolutional neural network for graph-structured data with Yotam Hechtlinger and Jining Qin.
